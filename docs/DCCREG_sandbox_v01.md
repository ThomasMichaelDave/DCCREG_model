## SEED BRANCH 1 — The "atmosphere": the medium-as-vacuum and its cosmological reach

*Origin: Block IV gravity-gate discussion. The disciplined core of this idea (a bounded screening length as a prediction; the boundary-condition section) is earmarked for **Block IV v0.4** and is NOT part of the sandbox. What lives here is only the speculative cosmological reach.*

**The reframe (the part that is [IR]-leaning, hence a promotion candidate, handled in Block IV):** GR assumes an empty Minkowski vacuum with asymptotic flatness. The DCCREG "vacuum" is instead the substrate's equilibrium — a pressurised, chiral, plasma-like fluid with a preferred frame and a Planck floor. So the boundary conditions differ from GR's in three ways: a non-vanishing background ("atmosphere"), a plasma/penetration-depth **screening length** $\kappa$, and a preferred frame + UV floor. **Einstein gravity becomes the featureless-medium limit** (flat background, $\kappa\to0$, no frame, no floor) — a *doubly-exceptional corner*: the $\alpha_1,\alpha_2\to0$ coupling limit (IV-§6.7) **and** the no-atmosphere boundary-condition limit.

**The speculative reach (this is what stays in the sandbox, [RH]):**

- **S-1.1 — Screening → dark sector.** If gravity rides a screened propagator $1/(k^2+\kappa^2)$ (Yukawa, finite range), the long-range deviation from $1/r$ has the *shape* of the dark-sector anomalies — flat galactic rotation curves, cosmic acceleration. **[RH — suggestive only.** A screening length modifies gravity at one scale; rotation curves and acceleration are *different* scales and signs, so a single $\kappa$ almost certainly cannot do both. Most likely this is a false lead; logged so it can be ruled out cleanly rather than re-tempted.]
- **S-1.2 — The atmosphere as cosmological background.** The substrate equilibrium profile = a built-in cosmological background. Does its stratification / pressure profile play the role of a cosmological-constant-like term, or a time-dependent background (an emergent expansion)? **[RH]**
- **S-1.3 — Which sector is screened?** In a vortex lattice the penetration depth screens the compression/tilt moduli but *not* the local shear $C_{66}$. Light/gravity ride shear. So naively gravity is *unscreened* and $\kappa\to0$ — i.e. Einstein survives and the dark-sector reach evaporates. The escape: disclinations (the gravity-relevant defects) couple to the dilatational sector too, which *is* screened. Whether the gravity defects feel $\kappa$ is therefore the same defect-coupling question as Fork #1. **[RH, but tied to a real Block computation.]**

**Promotion status:** the screening-length-as-a-prediction is an [IR] candidate → Block IV v0.4 (disciplined). Everything in S-1.1/1.2 is [RH] and stays.

## SEED BRANCH 2 — "Einstein as exception" as a programme-wide stance

*Origin: the user's "atmosphere effect" intuition, generalised.*

**S-2.1.** The recurring pattern across Blocks III–IV is that the *standard relativistic result* keeps appearing as a **limit** of the DCCREG result, not as its generic case:
- Lorentz invariance is IR-exact, Planck-violated in the UV (III-§5).
- Isotropy is the natural $\beta=\alpha$ point, with residual anisotropy as a prediction (III-§6).
- GR is the $\alpha_1,\alpha_2\to0$ + featureless-medium corner (IV-§6.7, S-1).

**The speculative generalisation [RH]:** *every* exact symmetry of established physics is, in DCCREG, an emergent IR limit of a medium that generically breaks it slightly — and the small breakings are the framework's collected predictions. This is a *philosophy*, not a result; it could be a deep organising principle or a way of dressing up "we keep not quite reproducing the textbook." The sandbox is the right place to hold it until (if ever) it earns its keep by predicting a *specific* breaking that is then computed in a Block. **[RH — explicitly flagged as possibly just rationalisation; the test is whether it ever yields a number.]**
